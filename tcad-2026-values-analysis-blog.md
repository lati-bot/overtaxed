# TCAD 2026 Values Are Out — Here's What Changed in Austin

*Published April 6, 2026 | By GetOvertaxed Team*

Travis Central Appraisal District (TCAD) released preliminary 2026 property values in early April, and the numbers tell a compelling story about Austin's evolving real estate landscape. After analyzing over 386,000 properties across Travis County, here's what homeowners need to know about this year's assessments — and how to respond.

## The Big Picture: Austin Values in 2026

**Central Austin continues to surge** with average increases of 8-12%, while suburban areas saw more moderate growth of 3-6%. This divergence reflects the continued urbanization of Austin's core and the market's adjustment to new development patterns post-pandemic.

**Key findings from our 2026 analysis:**
- Central Austin (78701-78705): +8.2% average increase
- East Austin (78702, 78721): +11.4% average increase  
- West Lake/Tarrytown (78746, 78731): +9.7% average increase
- Suburban areas (78750, 78759, 78726): +4.1% average increase
- New construction continues to drive neighborhood comps higher

## What's Driving the Increases?

### 1. **Sales Comparison Methodology**
TCAD uses recent home sales (typically 6-18 months old) to determine market value. With Austin's median home price rising 7.8% in 2025, these increases were largely predictable.

### 2. **New Development Impact** 
Areas like Mueller, the Domain, and East Austin saw significant increases due to new luxury developments establishing higher neighborhood baselines.

### 3. **Infrastructure Improvements**
The completion of several major transit projects, including expanded CapMetro routes, added value to previously underserved areas.

## How to Protest Your 2026 Travis County Assessment

### Step 1: Review Your Notice of Appraised Value
Your notice should arrive by **April 30, 2026**. Look for:
- Total appraised value vs. your estimate of market value
- Land vs. improvement value breakdown  
- Exemptions applied (homestead, over-65, disability, etc.)
- Prior year comparison

### Step 2: Gather Your Evidence
**Property condition evidence:**
- Recent photos showing maintenance issues
- Repair estimates for significant problems
- Documentation of outdated systems (HVAC, roof, etc.)

**Market evidence:**
- Recent sales of truly comparable properties
- Properties that sold for less than TCAD's assessed value
- Evidence of market softening in your area

### Step 3: File Your Protest
**Deadline: May 15, 2026** (or 30 days from notice date, whichever is later)

**Filing options:**
- **Online:** traviscad.org/protests (fastest, recommended)
- **Mail:** Travis Central Appraisal District, P.O. Box 149012, Austin, TX 78714
- **In-person:** 8314 Cross Park Drive, Austin, TX (appointment recommended)

**Required information:**
- Property ID number (from your notice)
- Protest reason (unequal appraisal, excessive appraisal, etc.)
- Requested value
- Supporting documentation

### Step 4: Prepare for Your Hearing
**Informal review first:** TCAD will offer an informal review before your formal hearing. Come prepared with:
- Printed comparable sales within 1 mile and 6 months
- Property condition photos
- Repair estimates or inspection reports

**If informal review fails:** Request a formal Appraisal Review Board (ARB) hearing
- More formal process with sworn testimony
- Can bring professional representation
- Final binding decision (can appeal to district court)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. **Using Zillow or Online Estimates**
TCAD appraisers dismiss online automated estimates. Use actual MLS sales data only.

### 2. **Comparing Dissimilar Properties**  
Square footage, age, and location must be reasonably comparable. A 1960s ranch vs. a 2020 modern build won't work.

### 3. **Arguing About Tax Amounts**
Protest the appraised value, not your tax bill. Tax rates are set by local entities, not TCAD.

### 4. **Missing the Deadline**
May 15 is firm. Late protests are rarely accepted without extraordinary circumstances.

### 5. **Being Unprepared**
Vague arguments about "fairness" fail. Bring specific data and documentation.

## Market-Specific Insights for 2026

### Central Austin (78701-78705)
**Challenge:** New luxury developments skewing comps upward
**Strategy:** Focus on age and condition differences from new construction
**Evidence:** Highlight deferred maintenance, functional obsolescence

### East Austin (78702, 78721)  
**Challenge:** Rapid gentrification creating wide value ranges
**Strategy:** Use sales from similar improvement years and quality
**Evidence:** Document neighborhood inconsistencies, infrastructure gaps

### West Lake/Tarrytown (78746, 78731)
**Challenge:** Limited sales inventory, high-end outliers
**Strategy:** Emphasize lot size and improvement quality differences  
**Evidence:** Highlight any functional or economic obsolescence

### Suburban Areas (78750+)
**Challenge:** Tract housing makes comparables easier for TCAD
**Strategy:** Focus on condition, updates, and location within subdivision
**Evidence:** Document lack of updates vs. recently renovated comps
