The Gap
📊

Prediction platforms show prices, not analysis

Polymarket and Kalshi tell you what the market thinks — not why. No factor decomposition, no scenario modeling, no edge calculation.

🎰

Sports tools don't cover prediction markets

OddsJam, Action Network, Sharp — all strong in sports betting, zero coverage of political/crypto/macro prediction markets.

💡

No "what-if" modeling exists

Nobody lets you adjust factors with sliders and watch probability recalculate in real-time. This is the unique hook — a thinking tool for bettors.

🏦

"Bloomberg for prediction markets" doesn't exist

The financial world has Bloomberg, TradingView, Koyfin. The prediction market equivalent is wide open.

Final Verdict

✅ Strengths

  • Genuine gap — no cross-platform prediction analytics exists
  • Massive tailwind — 40x market growth, still early
  • Regulatory clarity (CFTC approvals)
  • Capital efficient — ~$2K to MVP
  • Multiple revenue paths (SaaS, API, data)
  • Expandable into sports betting
  • LLMs unlock the probability engine
  • Network effects possible

✗ Weaknesses

  • Weak initial moat — replicable concept
  • Small TAM today (~$50-100M)
  • Platform risk from Polymarket/Kalshi
  • "Beating the market" is genuinely hard
  • Name collision with Signal Messenger
  • Cyclical demand patterns
  • Requires ongoing model curation

Build It. Ship Before Midterms.

The window is open. 6 months to the 2026 midterm cycle. Cross-platform from day 1. Thinking tool, not oracle. Let the market validate.

But first — pick a real name 👇
