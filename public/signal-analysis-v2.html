The gap nobody's filling

Prediction platforms show you prices, not analysis. Polymarket and Kalshi tell you what the market thinks — not why. No factor decomposition, no scenario modeling, no edge calculation. You get a number and a chart. That's it.

Meanwhile, every sharp sports betting tool (OddsJam, Action Network) ignores prediction markets entirely. And there's no "what-if" modeling anywhere — nobody lets you adjust factors with sliders and watch probability recalculate in real-time. That's the hook: a thinking tool for bettors.

The financial world has Bloomberg, TradingView, Koyfin. The prediction market equivalent? Wide open.

The market is real — and moving fast

Polymarket did roughly $21.5B in volume in 2025. Up from nearly zero in 2023. Kalshi hit $17.1B after getting CFTC approval for election contracts. US sports betting alone: $167B wagered, $18B in revenue, growing at ~11% CAGR.

How the engine actually works

The model isn't trying to beat the market. It's trying to help you think about the market. That's an important distinction — FiveThirtyEight doesn't beat prediction markets either, and it built a massive audience.

LLM ReasoningGPT-4/Claude decomposes events into factors, estimates probabilitySecret Weapon

The interactive sliders are a thinking framework, not an oracle. That's valuable even if the baseline model just reflects market consensus.

Bottom line

Going for it

  • Genuine gap — no cross-platform prediction analytics exists
  • Massive tailwind — 40× market growth, still early
  • Regulatory clarity (CFTC approvals)
  • Capital efficient — ~$2K to MVP
  • Multiple revenue paths (SaaS, API, data)
  • Expandable into sports betting
  • LLMs unlock the probability engine
  • Network effects possible

Against it

  • Weak initial moat — replicable concept
  • Small TAM today (~$50–100M)
  • Platform risk from Polymarket/Kalshi
  • "Beating the market" is genuinely hard
  • Name collision with Signal Messenger
  • Cyclical demand patterns
  • Requires ongoing model curation

Build it. Ship before midterms.

The window is open. Six months to the 2026 midterm cycle. Cross-platform from day one. Thinking tool, not oracle. Let the market validate.
