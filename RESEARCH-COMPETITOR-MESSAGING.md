## 1. Ownwell (ownwell.com) ‚Äî Biggest Name in DIY/Hybrid

- Average savings stats prominently displayed
- GLBA compliance (financial data protection)
- Referral program ($20 credits)

**Key Claims:**
- Average customer who saves earns ~$774 annually
- Covers TX, CA, NY, FL, WA, GA (multi-state)
- Savings calculator tool

**CTA Language:** Address entry field (instant engagement), "savings calculator"

**What They Do Better:** Multi-state coverage, polished tech-forward brand, massive scale, savings calculator as lead gen tool, strong SEO/brand recognition

**What We Could Do Better:** More transparent about actual fee percentage upfront, more personal service, lower fees

## 2. O'Connor & Associates (poconnor.com) ‚Äî Largest TX Firm

**Trust Signals:**
- "Saved clients over $213 million in property taxes in 2025"
- "One of the nation's top property tax consulting firms"
- Decades of experience implied
- Client portal for status tracking

**Key Claims:**
- $213M+ saved in 2025
- More aggressive and effective than any other firm in TX
- All 3 phases of appeal covered
- Automatic year-over-year enrollment

**CTA Language:** Sign-up focused, deadline-driven urgency (county deadline tables)

**What They Do Better:** Judicial appeals and binding arbitration capability, massive scale, $213M savings claim, comprehensive 3-phase approach

**What We Could Do Better:** 50% contingency is very high ‚Äî easy to beat on price. Website is dated and text-heavy. No modern UX.

## Compiled Analysis

### Common Messaging Patterns Across All Competitors

1. **"You're overpaying"** ‚Äî Universal opener. Everyone frames it as the homeowner being overcharged.
2. **"No risk / no upfront cost"** ‚Äî Contingency firms lead with this. Flat-fee firms counter by showing contingency costs more long-term.
3. **"We do the work for you"** ‚Äî Convenience is the #1 selling point for full-service firms.
4. **"Technology + expertise"** ‚Äî Nearly everyone claims proprietary tech/data/algorithms.
5. **"Year-over-year automatic"** ‚Äî Lock-in positioning as a feature, not a trap.
6. **"X million saved"** ‚Äî Aggregate savings numbers as proof of scale.
7. **Address entry as first CTA** ‚Äî Low-friction entry point across most modern competitors.

**Quadrant Summary:**
- **Premium Full-Service:** O'Connor, Gill Denson, Texas Tax Protest ‚Äî high contingency, do everything
- **Tech-Enabled Full-Service:** Ownwell ‚Äî lower contingency, modern UX, scale play
- **Honest Moderate:** Home Tax Shield ‚Äî realistic expectations, moderate pricing
- **Budget DIY:** AppealDesk, SquareDeal ‚Äî flat fee, you file yourself

### Strategic Implications for Overtaxed

**Biggest opportunity:** There's a clear gap between AppealDesk ($49 DIY packet) and Ownwell (25% contingency full-service). A service that offers **real representation at a flat fee or low contingency** (e.g., 10-15% or $149-299 flat) could own the "best of both worlds" position.

**Messaging angles to own:**
1. **Radical transparency** ‚Äî show the math, show the process, show what others charge
2. **New homeowner focus** ‚Äî untapped audience nobody targets
3. **Neighborhood intelligence** ‚Äî "see how your street compares"
4. **True cost of inaction** ‚Äî compound loss calculator
5. **Human + tech at fair price** ‚Äî not a $49 packet, not a 50% haircut
